# Skybit — Story Bible

*"Pip the Punctual: A Skybit Express Story"*

---

## The Premise

Skybit Express is the only courier service that delivers between the
cloud-temples of the Forgotten Sky. The route is the gap between two pillars.
The flight plan is "yes." The dress code is "goggles, attitude."

**Pip** is their best courier. Pip is also their *only* courier.

Every morning, Pip clips on the aviator goggles, picks up a parcel from his
boss — a smug, perpetually-disappointed pelican named **Mr. Garrick** — and
takes off. Every coin is a tip from a satisfied client. Every pillar is
somebody's mailbox. Every storm is "a brief weather event we are not refunding
for."

Pip *would* clock off, but Mr. Garrick keeps squawking new addresses into his
earpiece. Sunset? "One more drop, Pip." Midnight? "Express priority, Pip."
Sunrise? *"Good morning, Pip."*

Today's delivery list has no bottom. Pip flies on. The tips are great.

---

## Why It Fits Skybit

| Game element | Story reading |
|---|---|
| Aviator sunglasses | Standard-issue Skybit Express uniform |
| Golden coins | Tips from grateful clients |
| Stone pillars | Mailboxes — each one is somebody's home |
| 8-phase day/night cycle | One very long shift; Garrick keeps adding orders |
| 3× mushroom power-up | *Sky-Spice Energy Bar* (from Garrick's emergency drawer) |
| Coin magnet | *Express Tip-Jar™* — patent-pending magnetized lapel pin |
| Time-slow charm | *Oat-Milk Smoothie* — slows the world while Pip sips |
| Crash into a pillar | Pip dropped the parcel. The ultimate courier disgrace. |
| Combo multiplier | The tip jar is *singing* |

The tone is warm, gently absurd, zero peril. Pip *loves* this job. He just
wishes Mr. Garrick would stop talking.

## Cast

### Pip
Scarlet macaw. Bird-class courier. Cheerful, slightly caffeinated. Goggles
two sizes too big. Flies first, reads the address later. Genuinely happy at
work.

### Mr. Garrick
Pelican. Pip's boss. Permanently dissatisfied. Communicates exclusively via
crackly earpiece. Has never personally delivered a parcel. Owns three
clipboards.

### The Clientele
Eight neighborhoods of weird sky-folk who order weird sky-things. (See
roster below.)

---

## Skybit Express Client Roster

One per biome phase. Tied to the existing day-night cycle so the world feels
populated as Pip flies.

| Phase | Region | Who lives there | What they order |
|---|---|---|---|
| Day | **Coconut Coast** | sun-bleached parrots in beach huts | sunscreen, mango chutney, more chutney |
| Golden Hour | **Amber Cliffs** | retired honey-badger librarians | overdue-book reminders |
| Sunset | **Rose Ruins** | very dramatic moths | "anything pink, no pressure" |
| Dusk | **Stormwatch** | weather monks who refuse umbrellas | umbrellas |
| Night | **The Black Reach** | a single insomniac owl | hot soup, every night, never explains |
| Predawn | **Lantern Vigil** | candle-makers running late for sunrise | wax, panic |
| Sunrise | **First Light** | yoga-class flamingos | matcha, only matcha |
| (loop) | back to **Coconut Coast** | the parrots forgot they already ordered | more chutney |

## Mr. Garrick — Earpiece Chatter

Short barks Pip hears while flying. Tone: a pelican who has never been wrong
about anything.

- *"Pip. Pip. Are you flying. You should be flying."*
- *"That was a near miss. We don't get paid for near misses."*
- *"The client is asking about the parcel. The client is always asking about the parcel."*
- *"Pip. Garrick. Over. Tip jar status?"*
- *"I'm not saying faster. I'm saying — yes, faster."*
- *"Lovely sunset. Don't look at it."*
- *"Storm clouds ahead. Skybit Express does not, legally, recognize storms."*
- *"Pip, the owl wants soup again. Yes, again. No, I don't know either."*
- *"Two more drops. Three more drops. Ignore the previous count."*

---

## Pip — Inner Monologue

Short thoughts Pip might think mid-flight. Tone: a bird who's genuinely happy
at work and slightly underslept.

- *"Pillar. Pillar. Coin. Pillar. Living the dream."*
- *"Garrick says faster. Garrick always says faster. Wings say maybe."*
- *"This soup is going to be cold and I am going to feel terrible about it."*
- *"Tip. Tip. Tip. Tip. Tip. Tip. Bonus tip. TIP."*
- *"The goggles fit better when I think about lunch."*
- *"Two pillars. Could be a doorway. Probably just two pillars."*
- *"That cloud looked at me funny."*

## Game-Over Framing

When Pip clips a pillar, he didn't *die*. **He dropped the parcel.** That's
the joke and the framing.

Sample sub-lines for a future game-over flavor pool:

- *"Pip dropped the parcel."*
- *"Mr. Garrick is on the line."*
- *"Refund issued. Try again."*
- *"The client has left a review."*
- *"Pip dusts off the goggles."*
- *"Tomorrow, then. Or in fifteen seconds. Same thing."*

For a future README hook, app-store blurb, or wherever one sentence is asked

> *Skybit — fly the sky's rudest mail route, tip-jar first.*

## Design Notes

This bible is intentionally small. Skybit is a casual pick-up-and-play game;
the story should be discovered, not narrated. Future surfaces — if and when —
could include:

- A title-screen tagline rotation drawing from the marketing line.
- A tiny "now delivering to **Amber Cliffs**" toast on biome change.
- A "PIP'S LOG" line on the run-summary screen, gated by score milestone.
- Rare flavor float-text on coin pickup (*"piece o' eight!"* → *"big tip!"*).
- A game-over flavor pool drawn from the list above.

None of that is required. The story works the moment a player learns Pip's
name and what Mr. Garrick sounds like.
